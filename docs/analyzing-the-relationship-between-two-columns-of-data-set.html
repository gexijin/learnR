flattenCorrMatrix <- function(cormat, pmat) {
  ut <- upper.tri(cormat)
  data.frame(
    row = rownames(cormat)[row(cormat)[ut]],
    column = rownames(cormat)[col(cormat)[ut]],
    cor  =(cormat)[ut],
    p = pmat[ut]
    )
}

From the pearson and spearman correlation matrix, we find that there are:

Positive correlationship between HS.Grad and Income and Life.Exp with r value of 0.62 and 0.58, that means the higher education, the more income and longer life expectation;

Positive correlationship between Murder and Illiteracy, with r value of 0.70? It’s interesting; Negative correlation between Murder and Life.Exp, Frost, with r value of -0.78, and -0.54. It’s unreasonable that the more accurance of murder, the shorter life expectation; And the colder of the weather, the lower number of murder: too cold to murder!!!

Illiteracy has negative correlationship with Frost, HS.Grad and Life.Exp, with r value of -0.67, -0.66 and -0.59 respectly. The high percentage of Illiteracy, the lower percentage of high school graduation and less life expectation? The warmer it was, the higher enthusism to read and write?

When we check the siganificance of the correlationship, we find p value is less than 0.05 for every couple of combination we mentioned above. So all these correlations are 95% statistially significant!
